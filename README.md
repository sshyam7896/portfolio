# Shyam Patel's Professional Portfolio

Welcome to my GitHub repository where I showcase my extensive portfolio of projects spanning bioinformatics, data science, and computational biology. Below you'll find detailed information about each project, focusing on the technologies used, key learnings, and impactful outcomes.

---

## Capstone Project - DermaMetrics

### Technologies Used
This project leveraged cutting-edge sensor technology and neurostimulation techniques aimed at innovating chronic wound care. The use of advanced biomedical devices enabled real-time monitoring and treatment, providing data-driven insights into wound healing processes.

### Learnings
Throughout this project, I developed skills in product design, research and development, and corporate strategy formulation. As a co-founder and CTO, I learned how to effectively lead a team in a highly interdisciplinary environment, ensuring the integration of clinical insights with engineering innovation.

### Outcomes
DermaMetrics successfully developed and pitched a viable product that addresses a critical need in healthcare. The project not only established a foundational strategy for clinical trials but also set the stage for future market expansion, securing interest from potential investors and stakeholders in the healthcare industry.

---

## Data Analyst Externship at Griffiss Institute

### Technologies Used
I utilized Tableau and various statistical analysis methodologies to process and analyze demographic data effectively. These tools allowed for the visualization of complex datasets and the extraction of meaningful patterns, supporting robust data-driven decisions.

### Learnings
This externship enhanced my capabilities in leading analytics projects, where I learned to distill large sets of data into actionable insights. The experience sharpened my skills in data visualization and statistical inference, crucial for strategic planning in educational and governmental settings.

### Outcomes
The analytics work conducted during this externship provided significant enhancements to the diversity initiatives within the VICEROY program, influencing policy decisions and supporting the development of more inclusive STEM education programs.

---

## Python Project – Rutgers ClimateTracker

### Technologies Used
This project was built using Python, with extensive use of libraries for data processing like Pandas and visualization tools such as Matplotlib and Seaborn. Custom data structures were designed to handle the large volumes of historical climate data efficiently. 

### Learnings
Developing the Rutgers ClimateTracker honed my skills in handling big datasets and implementing algorithms tailored to the analysis and visualization of environmental data. The project improved my proficiency in Python and introduced me to innovative data structuring and processing techniques.

### Outcomes
The ClimateTracker has provided valuable insights into historical climate patterns, offering a detailed understanding of climate variability over more than a century. This tool has become a resource for researchers and the public, aiding in the education and awareness of climate change issues.

---

## Statistical Analyst – NBA Regression Analysis

### Technologies Used
Utilized R to conduct multiple regression analysis, focusing on various performance metrics as predictors for NBA team victories. The use of predictive analytics and data modeling techniques allowed for a comprehensive analysis of team performance data. 

### Learnings
This project deepened my understanding of statistical models in sports analytics, teaching me how to apply regression analysis to predict outcomes based on performance indicators. It also refined my ability to interpret and communicate statistical results to inform strategic decisions in sports management.

### Outcomes
The regression analysis provided key insights into the factors most significantly affecting NBA team successes, helping team strategists to fine-tune their approaches to player performance and game strategy. The findings from this project have been considered for publications and presentations in sports analytics conferences.

---
